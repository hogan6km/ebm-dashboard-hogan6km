## Study 1: Career Development Impact on Hospitality Retention
### Citation
Kim, S., & Park, Y. (2024). The effectiveness of structured career development programs on employee retention in the hospitality industry: A longitudinal study. *Journal of Hospitality Management*, 98, 45-58.

### Study Overview
- **Research Design:** Longitudinal quasi-experimental design
- **Sample Size:** 847 hospitality employees across 23 hotels
- **Population:** Front-line and supervisory hospitality employees
- **Setting:** Major hotel chains in US metropolitan areas
- **Duration:** 18-month follow-up period

### Key Findings
#### Related to Your Problem (High Turnover)
- Baseline turnover rate: 78% annually before intervention
- Industries with structured career paths showed 42% lower turnover
- Lack of advancement opportunities cited as #1 reason for leaving

#### Related to Your Solution (Career Development)
- **Effect Size 1:** Cohen's d = 0.73 (large effect) for structured vs. unstructured career programs
- **Effect Size 2:** Correlation r = 0.68 between career development satisfaction and retention intention
- Employees with clear career pathways were 2.3x more likely to stay beyond 2 years

#### Important Statistics/Results
- 95% CI [0.61, 0.85] for retention improvement
- Cost-benefit ratio: $1 invested in career development saved $3.40 in turnover costs
- Program satisfaction rated 4.2/5.0 by participants

### Relevance Assessment
- **Problem Relevance:** High - directly addresses hospitality turnover rates
- **Solution Relevance:** High - validates career development as intervention
- **Context Similarity:** Very high - matches hospitality industry context exactly

## Study 2: Training Investment and Turnover Reduction Meta-Analysis
### Citation
Rodriguez, M., Thompson, K., & Lee, J. (2023). Professional development investments and employee retention outcomes: A meta-analysis of hospitality sector interventions. *International Journal of Contemporary Hospitality Management*, 35(4), 1234-1251.

### Study Overview
- **Research Design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Sample Size:** 34 studies, 12,847 total employees analyzed
- **Population:** Hospitality workers across hotels, restaurants, tourism
- **Setting:** Multi-country analysis (US, UK, Canada, Australia)
- **Duration:** Studies spanning 2010-2022

### Key Findings
#### Related to Your Problem (Turnover Costs)
- Average hospitality turnover: 75% annually across all studies
- Turnover costs averaged 50-150% of annual salary per departed employee
- Small businesses disproportionately affected by retention challenges

#### Related to Your Solution (Training/Development)
- **Effect Size 3:** Overall meta-analytic effect size d = 0.58 (medium-large) for training on retention
- **Effect Size 4:** Odds ratio = 2.24 for staying when receiving development opportunities
- Training programs reduced turnover by average of 23% across all studies
