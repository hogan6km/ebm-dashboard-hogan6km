# Stakeholder mapping + Power/Interest analysis as required for assignment

## Stakeholder Evidence Goals 
**Primary Goal:** Map stakeholder perspectives on hospitality turnover impact and career development solutions
**Secondary Goal:** Assess stakeholder power/influence for successful implementation of retention programs

## STAKEHOLDER MAPPING - MILESTONE 2 REQUIREMENT ✅

### Primary Stakeholders (Direct Impact/High Interest)
#### Business Owners/Management  
- **Target Participants:** Restaurant/hotel owners, general managers, operations directors
- **Engagement Method:** Semi-structured interviews (30-45 minutes)
- **Key Questions Focus:** Financial impact of turnover, ROI expectations for career development, implementation concerns
- **Expected Response Rate:** 75% (5-7 participants from local hospitality association)

#### Front-line Employees
- **Target Participants:** Servers, housekeeping, front desk, kitchen staff (all experience levels)
- **Engagement Method:** Anonymous surveys + voluntary focus groups  
- **Key Questions Focus:** Career aspirations, barriers to advancement, willingness to participate in development
- **Expected Response Rate:** 60% survey, 15% focus group participation

#### HR/Training Managers
- **Target Participants:** Human resource managers, training coordinators, department supervisors
- **Engagement Method:** In-depth interviews (45-60 minutes)
- **Key Questions Focus:** Current training practices, resource constraints, program design preferences
- **Expected Response Rate:** 80% (specialized role, high engagement expected)

### Secondary Stakeholders (Indirect Impact/Moderate Interest)
#### Customers/Guests
- **Target Participants:** Repeat customers, business travelers, leisure guests
- **Engagement Method:** Brief surveys (5-10 questions), feedback forms
- **Key Questions Focus:** Service quality expectations, impact of staff turnover on experience
- **Expected Response Rate:** 25% (incentivized participation)

#### Industry Suppliers/Partners
- **Target Participants:** Staffing agencies, training vendors, hospitality service providers
- **Engagement Method:** Informal interviews, partnership discussions
- **Key Questions Focus:** Industry trends, solution offerings, collaboration opportunities  
- **Expected Response Rate:** 70% (business development interest)

## POWER/INTEREST DIAGRAM ANALYSIS - MILESTONE 2 REQUIREMENT ✅

### High Power, High Interest (MANAGE CLOSELY)
- **Business Owners:** Ultimate decision authority, direct financial impact
- **General Managers:** Implementation responsibility, operational impact  
- **HR Directors:** Program design and execution ownership

### High Power, Low Interest (KEEP SATISFIED)  
- **Corporate Leadership:** Policy approval authority but limited day-to-day involvement
- **Financial Officers:** Budget approval but focused on ROI metrics only
- **Legal Counsel:** Compliance oversight, program review authority

### Low Power, High Interest (KEEP INFORMED)
- **Front-line Employees:** Direct program beneficiaries but limited decision influence
- **Department Supervisors:** Local implementation support but limited authority
- **Training Coordinators:** Program execution support, subject matter expertise

### Low Power, Low Interest (MONITOR)
- **Customers/Guests:** Indirect impact recipients, limited influence on internal programs
- **Industry Suppliers:** Potential solution providers but external to decision process
- **Regulatory Bodies:** Compliance monitoring but passive involvement

## Stakeholder Engagement Timeline - SYSTEMATIC APPROACH ✅

### Phase 1: High Power Stakeholders (Weeks 1-2)
- Individual interviews with owners/GM's to assess support and concerns
- Present initial research findings and solution framework
- Negotiate resource allocation and success metrics

### Phase 2: High Interest Stakeholders (Weeks 3-4)  
- Employee surveys and focus groups to assess receptivity
- HR manager workshops to design implementation approach
- Supervisor briefings on program requirements and benefits

### Phase 3: Secondary Stakeholders (Weeks 5-6)
- Customer feedback collection on service quality expectations
- Supplier consultations on available training solutions
- Industry benchmarking through association networks
