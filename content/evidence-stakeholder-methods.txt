# Stakeholder Evidence: Methods & Engagement Strategy 
# Stakeholder mapping + Power/Interest analysis as required for assignment

## Stakeholder Evidence Goals 
**### Informed Consent
- **Purpose Explanation:** "This research aims to identify factors affecting employee retention and develop evidence-based solutions that improve job satisfaction and organizational effectiveness for all stakeholders."
- **Participation Voluntariness:** "Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may skip any questions you're uncomfortable answering and can withdraw from the study at any time without any consequences."
- **Data Use:** "Responses will be analyzed to identify patterns and develop retention improvement recommendations. Individual responses will be confidential and data will be reported in aggregate form only."
- **Anonymity/Confidentiality:** "Individual responses will not be shared with management. All data will be stored securely and participants can choose to remain completely anonymous or provide contact information for follow-up only if desired."

### Approvals & Permissions
- **Supervisor Approval:** Yes - need written management approval for employee surveys/interviews including clear communication about confidentiality protections and voluntary participation
- **HR Consultation:** Essential - coordinate with HR to ensure compliance with company policies, union agreements (if applicable), and appropriate communication channels
- **External Stakeholder Protocols:** Customer contact requires management approval and standardized approach ensuring professional representation and minimal business disruptional:** Map stakeholder perspectives on hospitality turnover impact and career development solutions
**Secondary Goal:** Assess stakeholder power/influence for successful implementation of retention programs

## STAKEHOLDER MAPPING 

### Primary Stakeholders (Direct Impact/High Interest)
#### Business Owners/Management  
- **Target Participants:** Restaurant/hotel owners, general managers, operations directors
- **Engagement Method:** Semi-structured interviews (30-45 minutes)
- **Key Questions Focus:** Financial impact of turnover, ROI expectations for career development, implementation concerns
- **Expected Response Rate:** 75% (5-7 participants from local hospitality association)

#### Front-line Employees
- **Target Participants:** Servers, housekeeping, front desk, kitchen staff (all experience levels)
- **Engagement Method:** Anonymous surveys + voluntary focus groups  
- **Key Questions Focus:** Career aspirations, barriers to advancement, willingness to participate in development
- **Expected Response Rate:** 60% survey, 15% focus group participation

#### HR/Training Managers
- **Target Participants:** Human resource managers, training coordinators, department supervisors
- **Engagement Method:** In-depth interviews (45-60 minutes)
- **Key Questions Focus:** Current training practices, resource constraints, program design preferences
- **Expected Response Rate:** 80% (specialized role, high engagement expected)

### Secondary Stakeholders (Indirect Impact/Moderate Interest)
#### Customers/Guests
- **Target Participants:** Repeat customers, business travelers, leisure guests
- **Engagement Method:** Brief surveys (5-10 questions), feedback forms
- **Key Questions Focus:** Service quality expectations, impact of staff turnover on experience
- **Expected Response Rate:** 25% (incentivized participation)

#### Industry Suppliers/Partners
- **Target Participants:** Staffing agencies, training vendors, hospitality service providers
- **Engagement Method:** Informal interviews, partnership discussions
- **Key Questions Focus:** Industry trends, solution offerings, collaboration opportunities  
- **Expected Response Rate:** 70% (business development interest)

## POWER/INTEREST DIAGRAM ANALYSIS 

### High Power, High Interest (MANAGE CLOSELY)
- **Business Owners:** Ultimate decision authority, direct financial impact
- **General Managers:** Implementation responsibility, operational impact  
- **HR Directors:** Program design and execution ownership

### High Power, Low Interest (KEEP SATISFIED)  
- **Corporate Leadership:** Policy approval authority but limited day-to-day involvement
- **Financial Officers:** Budget approval but focused on ROI metrics only
- **Legal Counsel:** Compliance oversight, program review authority

### Low Power, High Interest (KEEP INFORMED)
- **Front-line Employees:** Direct program beneficiaries but limited decision influence
- **Department Supervisors:** Local implementation support but limited authority
- **Training Coordinators:** Program execution support, subject matter expertise

### Low Power, Low Interest (MONITOR)
- **Customers/Guests:** Indirect impact recipients, limited influence on internal programs
- **Industry Suppliers:** Potential solution providers but external to decision process
- **Regulatory Bodies:** Compliance monitoring but passive involvement

## Stakeholder Engagement Timeline 

### Phase 1: High Power Stakeholders (Weeks 1-2)
- Individual interviews with owners/GM's to assess support and concerns
- Present initial research findings and solution framework
- Negotiate resource allocation and success metrics

### Phase 2: High Interest Stakeholders (Weeks 3-4)  
- Employee surveys and focus groups to assess receptivity

- Customer feedback collection on service quality expectations

## Data Collection Methods
### Survey Design
#### Online Survey Platform
- **Platform Choice:** SurveyMonkey Professional for advanced analytics and anonymity features
- **Survey Length:** 10-15 minutes completion time to maximize response rates
- **Question Types:** Mix of 5-point Likert scales, multiple choice, ranking questions, and 2-3 open-ended responses
- **Incentives:** Entry into gift card drawing ($50 value), summary report of findings, recognition in final project

#### Key Survey Questions
**Problem Assessment Questions:**
- How frequently do you encounter employee turnover challenges in your role?
- How significantly does high turnover impact your work effectiveness and customer service quality?
- What do you see as the main causes of employee retention problems in retail?

**Solution Feedback Questions:**
- How would you rate the feasibility of implementing enhanced manager training for retention?
- What concerns do you have about managers spending more time on employee development?
- What would make retention training programs more effective in your organization?

**Priority and Support Questions:**
- How important is solving this problem compared to other priorities?
- What level of support would you provide for this initiative?
- What resources should be allocated to address this problem?

### Interview Protocol
#### Interview Structure
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes to allow thorough exploration while respecting busy schedules
- **Format:** Video conference preferred for convenience, in-person for key stakeholders when possible
- **Recording:** Audio recording with consent for accuracy, backed up by detailed notes
- **Note-taking:** Real-time structured notes using interview template, immediate post-interview summary

#### Interview Questions
**Opening Questions:**
- Tell me about your role and how this problem affects you
- How long have you been aware of this issue?

**Problem Exploration:**
- Can you describe a specific example of when this problem occurred?
- What impact has this problem had on your work/experience?
- What do you think causes this problem?

**Solution Discussion:**
- What solutions have you considered or tried for retention challenges?
- How do you think enhanced manager training for retention would work in practice?
- What challenges or barriers do you foresee in implementing retention programs?

**Implementation Questions:**
- What would success look like from your perspective (reduced turnover, improved satisfaction)?
- What support would you need to make retention training programs work effectively?
- Who else should be involved in addressing employee retention challenges?

### Focus Groups (if applicable)
#### Focus Group Design
- **Group Size:** 6-8 participants per group for optimal discussion dynamics
- **Number of Groups:** 3 focus groups (managers, front-line employees, customers)
- **Group Composition:** Homogeneous groups by role to encourage open discussion
- **Facilitation:** Trained facilitator using structured discussion guide with probing questions

## Sampling Strategy

### Stakeholder Identification
#### Stakeholder Mapping
**Internal Stakeholders:** Store managers (5), department supervisors (10), front-line employees (25), HR staff (3)
**External Stakeholders:** Regular customers (15), key suppliers (3), industry peers (5)
**Leadership:** Senior management team (4), operations director, finance director

#### Sampling Approach
- **Total Target Sample:** 75 stakeholders across all categories
- **Sampling Method:** Purposive sampling for managers/leadership, stratified random sampling for employees by department
- **Representation Goals:** Include perspectives from all departments, tenure levels, shifts, and customer segments

### Recruitment Strategy
#### Outreach Method
**Email Invitation Template:**
"We're conducting research to improve employee retention and job satisfaction in our organization. Your perspective as a sales associate is valuable to understanding the challenges we face and developing effective solutions. This survey/interview will take approximately 20-30 minutes and your responses will help shape retention strategies that benefit everyone. Your participation is voluntary and responses can be kept confidential if preferred."

**Follow-up Strategy:**
Initial contact via company email/preferred communication channel, first follow-up after 5 business days with simplified participation request, second follow-up after 10 days offering alternative participation methods (shorter survey, brief phone call). Final reminder 3 days before survey close with emphasis on impact and appreciation.

**Gatekeepers:**
Obtain management endorsement and HR department support for employee outreach, coordinate with department heads for customer contact protocols, work with union representatives where applicable, and ensure compliance with organizational communication policies.

## Timeline and Logistics

### Data Collection Schedule
- **Survey Launch:** Week 3 of project after management approvals and pilot testing completed
- **Survey Close:** 3 weeks after launch to allow adequate response time and follow-up reminders
- **Interview Period:** Weeks 4-6 allowing flexibility for participant scheduling and comprehensive data collection
- **Focus Group Dates:** Week 5-6 after initial survey analysis to explore emerging themes and validate findings
- **Follow-up Period:** Week 7 for clarification questions and participant validation of preliminary findings

### Logistical Planning
#### Survey Distribution
- **Distribution Method:** Company email system with link to secure online survey platform (SurveyMonkey/Qualtrics) plus paper copies for employees without regular email access
- **Reminder Schedule:** Initial invitation, reminder at 1 week, second reminder at 2 weeks, final reminder 3 days before close with response rate updates
- **Technical Support:** IT help desk contact information provided, alternative phone/paper completion options, dedicated email for survey questions and technical issues

#### Interview Scheduling
- **Scheduling Method:** Online scheduling system (Calendly) with multiple time slots, phone scheduling for those preferring direct contact, flexible scheduling including breaks and after-hours options
- **Location Planning:** Private conference rooms for in-person interviews, secure video call platform for remote interviews, neutral locations for customer interviews
- **Equipment Needs:** Digital recording device with backup, structured interview guides, consent forms, note-taking materials, and secure data storage protocols

## Response Rate Strategy

### Maximizing Participation
#### Engagement Tactics
- **Leadership Endorsement:** Obtain written management support statement emphasizing importance of employee input and commitment to act on findings
- **Clear Value Proposition:** "Your input will directly influence retention strategies that can improve your work environment, career development opportunities, and job satisfaction"
- **Convenient Participation:** Multiple survey access methods, flexible interview scheduling, brief participation options (5-minute mini-surveys), mobile-friendly formats
- **Multiple Touchpoints:** Department meetings to introduce research, follow-up emails, break room flyers, manager endorsements, peer-to-peer encouragement

#### Incentive Strategy
- **Direct Incentives:** Entry into gift card drawings, paid time for interview participation, refreshments during focus groups, early access to findings summary
- **Indirect Benefits:** Improved work environment, better management practices, enhanced development opportunities, stronger voice in organizational decisions
- **Recognition:** Thank you messages from leadership, acknowledgment in company newsletter, participant certificates, feedback on how input was used

### Minimum Viable Sample
- **Minimum Response Rate:** 40% employee response rate (60+ responses from 150 employees), representation from all departments and shift patterns for credible results
- **Backup Plan:** If response rates below 30%, extend survey period, offer paper alternatives, conduct brief interviews during shift changes, use department meetings for group feedback
- **Alternative Methods:** Focus groups during staff meetings, suggestion box feedback, informal conversations during management rounds, exit interview data analysis

## Data Management Plan

### Data Collection Organization
- **Response Tracking:** Secure spreadsheet with anonymous participant IDs, response dates, completion status, demographic categories (department, tenure) without identifying information
- **Data Storage:** Password-protected cloud storage (Google Drive/OneDrive) with restricted access, survey platform secure servers with automatic backup, physical documents in locked cabinet
- **Data Security:** All data anonymized immediately upon collection, no names linked to responses, aggregate reporting only, secure deletion of identifying information after analysis
- **Backup Plan:** Daily backups to separate cloud storage location, exported data files stored in multiple secure locations, paper backup of critical survey responses

### Analysis Preparation
- **Coding Strategy:** Thematic analysis of open-ended responses using inductive coding approach, multiple reviewers for reliability, focus on retention factors, satisfaction drivers, and improvement suggestions
- **Quantitative Analysis:** Descriptive statistics for all survey items, cross-tabulation by department and tenure, correlation analysis between satisfaction and retention intention, trend analysis over time
- **Integration Plan:** Triangulate survey data with interview insights and focus group themes, create stakeholder perspective matrix comparing different group viewpoints, develop comprehensive stakeholder evidence summary
