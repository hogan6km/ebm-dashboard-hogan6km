- **Data Source:** BLS QCEW, NAICS 721 (Accommodation)
- **Data Quality:** HIGH - Census of employment, 99% coverage

## Internal Reports Analysis

### Report 1: Annual Employee Engagement Survey Report
- **Report Period:** January 2024 - December 2024
- **Key Findings Related to Problem:** 42% of employees cite "lack of career development" as primary retention concern; manager relationship quality rated 5.8/10
- **Key Findings Related to Solution:** 78% of employees express interest in mentoring/coaching programs; 65% want more frequent manager feedback
- **Data Quality Assessment:** HIGH - 87% response rate, validated survey instrument, professional administration
- **Limitations:** Self-reported data subject to response bias; doesn't capture perspectives of departed employees

### Report 2: Quarterly Turnover Analysis Report
- **Report Period:** Q3-Q4 2024 
- **Key Findings Related to Problem:** Turnover concentrated in first 90 days (68% of departures); exit interviews cite "inadequate training/support" (45% of responses)
- **Key Findings Related to Solution:** Departments with structured mentoring show 25% lower turnover; new employee feedback requests more manager interaction
- **Data Quality Assessment:** GOOD - Based on HRIS data and structured exit interviews, 85% participation rate
- **Limitations:** Exit interview responses may be diplomatically filtered; doesn't capture long-term employee perspectives

### Report 3: Training Effectiveness Assessment
- **Report Period:** 2023-2024 training year
- **Key Findings Related to Problem:** Manager training completion at 60%; employee satisfaction with manager support correlates with training completion
- **Key Findings Related to Solution:** Completed training programs show 15% improvement in team retention; managers request more practical tools
- **Data Quality Assessment:** MODERATE - Training completion data reliable, outcome measures limited
- **Limitations:** Doesn't measure training quality or long-term retention of skills; self-reported effectiveness measures

## Process Analysis

### Current State Process Documentation
**Process Name:** Employee Onboarding and Development Process
- **Process Description:** 3-day orientation, assignment to manager, 30-60-90 day check-ins, performance review at 6 months
- **Process Performance:** 32% completion rate for full process, average time-to-productivity 8 weeks, manager participation inconsistent
- **Problem Manifestation:** 68% of early departures occur before 90-day process completion; manager check-ins completed only 45% of time
- **Improvement Potential:** Standardizing manager engagement could improve retention by estimated 20-25% based on pilot data

### Historical Process Performance
- **Baseline Performance:** 2020-2022: 75% process completion rate, 6-week time-to-productivity, 15% early departure rate
- **Performance Decline Pattern:** Steady decline starting mid-2022, accelerated in 2023 with staffing shortages affecting manager availability
- **Contributing Factors:** Increased workload limiting manager time, lack of accountability for process completion, inadequate manager training on retention
- **Previous Improvement Attempts:** Online training modules (limited adoption), mentoring pilot (positive results in 2 departments), manager incentives (discontinued due to budget)
